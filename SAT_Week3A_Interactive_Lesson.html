📐 Complete Formula Guide: When & How to Use Each One

Week 3 gave you all the tools. Week 3A teaches you exactly when to reach for each one. Click any formula card to expand the full strategy — including SAT-specific use cases and worked examples.

🎯 The SAT Formula Decision System

💡 MARIAH'S RULE: Before every math problem, spend 5 seconds asking: "What am I looking for?" The answer determines which formula you use. The 3 most common answers are: (1) a slope or rate of change → slope formula; (2) an equation of a line → point-slope or y = mx+b; (3) the solutions to a quadratic → factoring, formula, or Desmos. If the problem mentions "vertex," "maximum," or "minimum" → vertex form or axis of symmetry formula.

📋 LINEAR FUNCTIONS — Click Each Card to Expand

1. Slope Formula
m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)

WHAT IT FINDS: The slope (steepness / rate of change) between any two points.

🎯 USE WHEN: You're given two points (x₁,y₁) and (x₂,y₂) and need the slope OR when asked for a "rate of change."

⚠️ TRAP: Always label which point is #1 and #2 BEFORE subtracting. Mixing up order is the #1 arithmetic error on the SAT.

Points (−2, 3) and (4, 15):
m = (15 − 3) / (4 − (−2)) = 12 / 6 = 2
2. Slope-Intercept Form
y = mx + b

WHAT IT FINDS: The complete equation of a line. m = slope, b = y-intercept (where the line hits the y-axis).

🎯 USE WHEN: Asked to identify or write the equation of a line. Also use to instantly read slope and y-intercept from a given equation.

⚠️ TRAP: If the equation is in standard form (Ax + By = C), rewrite it to slope-intercept FIRST. Never read slope from standard form directly.

4x + 3y = 12 → y = −(4/3)x + 4
Slope = −4/3, y-intercept = 4
3. Point-Slope Form
y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)

WHAT IT FINDS: The equation of a line when given a slope and ONE point (not necessarily the y-intercept).

🎯 USE WHEN: You know the slope AND a point that is NOT (0, something). Point-slope is the fastest starting point — then simplify to slope-intercept at the end.

💡 PRO MOVE: If the given point IS (0, b), you already have the y-intercept — skip point-slope and go straight to y = mx + b.

m = 3/4, point (4, 1):
y − 1 = (3/4)(x − 4)
y = (3/4)x − 3 + 1 = (3/4)x − 2
4. Parallel & Perpendicular Lines
Parallel: m₁ = m₂ | Perp: m₁ × m₂ = −1

WHAT IT FINDS: The slope of a new line based on a given line's slope.

🎯 USE WHEN: The problem says "parallel to" → keep the exact same slope. "Perpendicular to" → flip and negate (negative reciprocal).

💡 NEGATIVE RECIPROCAL SHORTCUT: Flip the fraction, change the sign.
Slope = 4/3 → perpendicular slope = −3/4
Slope = −2 → perpendicular slope = 1/2

Line p has slope −4/3 → line perp. to p has slope 3/4
5. Systems of Equations — Special Solutions
No soln: same slope, diff. intercept
∞ solns: identical equations

WHAT IT FINDS: Whether a system has 0, 1, or infinitely many solutions.

🎯 USE WHEN: Asked "how many solutions does this system have?" or "for what value of k does this system have infinite solutions?"

💡 INFINITE SOLUTIONS HACK: One equation must be a scalar multiple of the other. Divide or multiply one equation to match the other, then solve for k.

3x − ky = 9 and 6x − 4y = 18
Divide eq.2 by 2: 3x − 2y = 9
Match: k = 2 → ∞ solutions

🔢 QUADRATIC FUNCTIONS — Click Each Card to Expand

6. Standard Form + Axis of Symmetry
f(x) = ax² + bx + c
Axis: x = −b / (2a)

WHAT IT FINDS: y-intercept (it's c), direction (up if a > 0, down if a < 0), and the x-value of the vertex.

🎯 USE WHEN: You need the vertex x-coordinate or the y-intercept. Also use to find the maximum/minimum of a real-world quadratic (e.g., projectile height).

💡 SHORTCUT: x = −b/(2a) gives you the x at the vertex. Plug that x back in to get the y (max or min value).

h(t) = −16t² + 64t + 5
t = −64/(2×(−16)) = 2
h(2) = −64 + 128 + 5 = 69 feet max
7. Vertex Form
f(x) = a(x − h)² + k
Vertex = (h, k)

WHAT IT FINDS: The vertex (h, k) directly. No calculation needed — just read it off!

🎯 USE WHEN: The equation is already in vertex form AND the SAT asks for the minimum value, maximum value, or vertex coordinates.

⚠️ TRAP: The sign on h is tricky. In (x − h)², if you see (x − 3)², then h = +3. If you see (x + 3)², that's (x − (−3))², so h = −3.

f(x) = 2(x − 3)² + 5
Vertex = (3, 5). Min value = 5 (since a=2 > 0)
8. Quadratic Formula
x = [−b ± √(b² − 4ac)] / 2a

WHAT IT FINDS: ALL solutions to any quadratic ax² + bx + c = 0. Always works, even when factoring fails.

🎯 USE WHEN: The quadratic does NOT factor cleanly, or when the answer choices contain radicals or fractions that suggest messy roots.

💡 BEFORE using the formula: Always rearrange to ax² + bx + c = 0 first. Then carefully identify a, b, and c.

2x² − 7x − 15 = 0: a=2, b=−7, c=−15
x = [7 ± √(49+120)] / 4 = [7 ± 13] / 4
x = 5 or x = −3/2
9. Discriminant
Δ = b² − 4ac
Δ > 0: two roots | Δ = 0: one root | Δ < 0: no real roots

WHAT IT FINDS: How many real solutions a quadratic has — WITHOUT solving it.

🎯 USE WHEN: Asked "how many solutions does f(x) have?" or "for what value of k does the equation have exactly one real solution?" → Set Δ = 0.

💡 ONE-SOLUTION TRICK: Δ = 0 → b² − 4ac = 0 → solve for the unknown variable (usually k).

x² + kx + 16 = 0 has 1 solution:
k² − 4(1)(16) = 0 → k² = 64 → k = ±8
10. Factored Form
f(x) = a(x − r₁)(x − r₂)
Roots: x = r₁ and x = r₂

WHAT IT FINDS: The x-intercepts (zeros/roots) of a quadratic directly — just read them off from the factored form.

🎯 USE WHEN: The equation factors cleanly (integers multiply to c, add to b) OR when you're given the roots and need to work backwards to the equation.

💡 BACKWARDS HACK: Given roots 3 and −5 → equation is a(x − 3)(x + 5). Expand and match any given information to find a.

x² + 7x + 12 = (x+3)(x+4)
Roots: x = −3 and x = −4

✏️ Formula Identification Practice — 4 Warm-Up Questions

Read each question. Identify the correct formula before choosing your answer.

Formula ID Question 1 of 4
A tutor charges a flat fee of $25 plus $15 per hour. Which formula form BEST models the total cost C as a function of hours h?
✅ Answer: B — Slope-Intercept Form
WHY B: The relationship is linear (constant rate of $15/hour + fixed $25). This matches y = mx + b:
• m = 15 (rate of change per hour)
• b = 25 (flat starting fee = y-intercept)
• So C = 15h + 25 ✓
❌ Why not D? Point-Slope gives the SAME equation but is not the "best model" — we have the y-intercept, so slope-intercept is cleaner and more direct. D is also technically correct as a starting point but B is the final, simplified form the SAT expects.
🗺️ Formula Signal: "flat fee" = y-intercept | "per hour" = slope → slope-intercept.
Formula ID Question 2 of 4
The SAT asks: "The function g(x) = −3(x + 2)² − 7. What is the maximum value of g(x)?" Which formula gives the answer most directly?
✅ Answer: C — Vertex Form
g(x) = −3(x + 2)² − 7 is already in vertex form: a(x − h)² + k
• Rewrite: g(x) = −3(x − (−2))² + (−7)
• So h = −2, k = −7, a = −3
• Since a = −3 < 0, parabola opens DOWN → vertex is the MAXIMUM
• Maximum value = k = −7, occurring at x = −2 ✓
You NEVER needed to expand or use the quadratic formula.
❌ Why not B? Axis of symmetry finds the x-value at the vertex — but you'd still need to substitute back in. Vertex form is one step faster.
Formula ID Question 3 of 4
The SAT asks: "For what value of k does 3x² − 6x + k = 0 have no real solutions?" Which tool gives the answer?
✅ Answer: B — Discriminant
"No real solutions" means discriminant < 0:
b² − 4ac < 0 where a = 3, b = −6, c = k
(−6)² − 4(3)(k) < 0
36 − 12k < 0
36 < 12k → k > 3 ✓
📌 Key Signal: "no real solutions" / "no real roots" → ALWAYS use the discriminant. No other formula answers this question.
Formula ID Question 4 of 4
Line ℓ₁ has equation 3x − 5y = 10. Line ℓ₂ is perpendicular to ℓ₁ and passes through (3, −1). To find the y-intercept of ℓ₂, which sequence of formulas is correct?
✅ Answer: B — The Multi-Step Linear Chain
Step 1: 3x − 5y = 10 → y = (3/5)x − 2. Slope of ℓ₁ = 3/5
Step 2: Perpendicular slope = −5/3 (negative reciprocal)
Step 3: Point-slope through (3, −1): y − (−1) = −(5/3)(x − 3)
y + 1 = −(5/3)x + 5 → y = −(5/3)x + 4
y-intercept = 4 ✓
💡 Chain Formula Problems are very common on SAT. The signal is "perpendicular to" + "passes through" + "y-intercept." Always go: slope → perpendicular slope → point-slope → simplify.

🔑 Linear Hacks — Shortcuts That Save 30–60 Seconds Each

🔴 HACK 1 — Free y-intercept: If any given point has x = 0, that point IS the y-intercept. Write b = y-value immediately. Skip all algebra.
💡 HACK 2 — Perpendicular slope in 3 seconds: Slope is 4/3? Flip → 3/4, change sign → −3/4. Done. Slope is −5? Write it as −5/1, flip → 1/5, change sign → +1/5.
🔵 HACK 3 — Infinite Solutions (find k): Divide the bigger equation to make it look like the smaller one. What's k? Whatever makes both equations identical.
🟢 HACK 4 — Word Problem Decoder:
"Each" / "per" / "every" → slope (m)
"Initial" / "starting" / "flat fee" / "deposit" → y-intercept (b)
"Total" / "after x units" → y-value to find
Once you label m and b, it's just y = mx + b.
🟣 HACK 5 — Standard Form Speed Read: For Ax + By = C:
• Slope = −A/B (don't expand, just negate and divide)
• y-intercept = C/B
• x-intercept = C/A

✏️ Linear Deep Practice — 6 SAT-Style Questions

Linear Question 1 of 6 — Slope & Parallel Lines
Line ℓ passes through (−2, 3) and (4, 15). Which is the equation of a line parallel to ℓ that passes through (0, −5)?
✅ Answer: B — y = 2x − 5
Step 1 — Find slope of ℓ: m = (15 − 3) / (4 − (−2)) = 12 / 6 = 2
Step 2 — Parallel means same slope: m = 2
Step 3 — Point (0, −5) has x = 0, so b = −5 directly!
Answer: y = 2x − 5 ✓
❌ Why not A (y = 3x − 5)? The slope calculation 12/6 = 2, NOT 3. This is a common arithmetic error — double-check by counting: from x=−2 to x=4 is 6 units across, not 4.
❌ Why not D (y = 2x + 5)? The slope is correct but the y-intercept is wrong. The point (0, −5) tells you b = −5, not +5.
Linear Question 2 of 6 — Infinite Solutions
For what value of k does the system 3x − ky = 9 and 6x − 4y = 18 have infinitely many solutions?
✅ Answer: B — k = 2
∞ Solutions = identical equations (same line)
Step 1 — Simplify equation 2: Divide 6x − 4y = 18 by 2 → 3x − 2y = 9
Step 2 — Compare to equation 1: 3x − ky = 9
Step 3 — Match coefficients: For identical equations, k must equal 2
k = 2 ✓
Verification: both equations become 3x − 2y = 9 (same line)
❌ Why not k = 4? k = 4 would give 3x − 4y = 9 and 3x − 2y = 9 — different y-coefficients → parallel lines with NO solution, not infinite solutions.
Linear Question 3 of 6 — Perpendicular Lines + y-intercept
Line p has equation 4x + 3y = 12. Line q is perpendicular to p and passes through (4, 1). What is the y-intercept of line q?
✅ Answer: B — y-intercept = −2
Step 1 — Find slope of p: 4x + 3y = 12 → 3y = −4x + 12 → y = −(4/3)x + 4. Slope of p = −4/3
Step 2 — Perpendicular slope: Flip and negate −4/3 → 3/4
Step 3 — Point-slope for q through (4, 1):
y − 1 = (3/4)(x − 4)
y − 1 = (3/4)x − 3
y = (3/4)x − 2
y-intercept = −2 ✓
❌ Why not 4? 4 is the y-intercept of LINE P (from 4x + 3y = 12 → y = −4/3 x + 4). The question asks for line q, not p.
❌ Why not 1? 1 is the y-coordinate of the given point (4, 1). The y-intercept is where x = 0, not the y from the given point.
Linear Question 4 of 6 — Word Problem (Rate + Starting Value)
A swimming pool contains 12,000 gallons. Water is draining at a constant rate of 400 gallons per hour. Which equation gives the number of gallons G remaining after t hours, and how many hours until the pool is empty?
✅ Answer: B — G = −400t + 12,000; empties at t = 30 hours
Decode the word problem:
• "draining" = slope is negative → m = −400
• "contains 12,000" = starting value = y-intercept → b = 12,000
Equation: G = −400t + 12,000
Pool empty when G = 0: 0 = −400t + 12,000 → 400t = 12,000 → t = 30 hours ✓
❌ Why not A? "Draining" means DECREASING — the slope must be negative. +400 means the pool is filling, not draining.
Linear Question 5 of 6 — System of Equations
A movie theater sells large popcorn for $8 and small popcorn for $5. In one hour, 35 items were sold for a total of $244. How many large popcorns were sold?
✅ Answer: C — 23 large popcorns
Let L = large, S = small
Equation 1 (count): L + S = 35
Equation 2 (revenue): 8L + 5S = 244
Substitution: S = 35 − L
8L + 5(35 − L) = 244
8L + 175 − 5L = 244
3L = 69 → L = 23 ✓
Check: 23 large + 12 small = 35. Revenue: 8(23) + 5(12) = 184 + 60 = 244 ✓
❌ Common trap: Always check by plugging back in. Getting L = 23 means S = 12. Verify BOTH equations before selecting your answer.
Linear Question 6 of 6 — Advanced: No Solution
The system kx + 3y = 6 and 2x + y = 4 has no solution. What is the value of k?
✅ Answer: C — k = 6
No solution = parallel lines = same slope, different y-intercept
Step 1 — Find slope of equation 2: 2x + y = 4 → y = −2x + 4. Slope = −2
Step 2 — Rewrite equation 1 in slope-intercept: kx + 3y = 6 → y = −(k/3)x + 2. Slope = −k/3
Step 3 — Set slopes equal (parallel): −k/3 = −2 → k = 6
Step 4 — Verify different intercepts: Line 1 has intercept 2, Line 2 has intercept 4. ✓ Different → no solution ✓
❌ Why not k = 3? k = 3 gives slope −3/3 = −1, but line 2 has slope −2. Different slopes → one solution (intersection), not no solution.

~4–5 SAT questions involve quadratics. This section attacks every question type with step-by-step solutions and hacks to solve them faster.

🛠️ The 5 Quadratic Question Types — Identified & Solved

Question TypeWhat to Look ForBest Method
Find the zeros/roots/solutions"What are the solutions?", "x-intercepts", "where f(x) = 0"Factor first; if messy → Quadratic Formula
Find vertex (min/max)"minimum value", "maximum height", "vertex"If in vertex form → read k. Otherwise → x = −b/(2a) then substitute

💡 REMEMBER THE RULE: a > 0 = parabola opens UP = vertex is MINIMUM. a < 0 = parabola opens DOWN = vertex is MAXIMUM. Check the sign of a FIRST before answering any min/max question.

🔑 Quadratic Hacks — The SAT Loves These

🔴 HACK 1 — Vertex Form Instant Read: If f(x) = a(x − h)² + k, then vertex = (h, k), min/max = k. Never expand vertex form to answer a vertex question. Read. It. Directly.
💡 HACK 2 — (b/2)² Shortcut for Completing the Square: Take the b coefficient, divide by 2, square it → that's the number you add to both sides. For x² + 8x + ? = complete square: add (8/2)² = 16. Result: (x+4)² = ...
🔵 HACK 3 — Discriminant for k problems: The phrase "exactly one real solution" → ALWAYS set b² − 4ac = 0 and solve. The phrase "no real solutions" → set b² − 4ac < 0 and find the range of k.
🟢 HACK 4 — Projectile Maximum Shortcut: For h(t) = −16t² + v₀t + h₀:
• Time of max height: t = v₀/(32) [since −b/(2a) = v₀/(32)]
• Max height: plug that t back in
• The h₀ term is just the starting height — it shifts the whole parabola up
🟣 HACK 5 — Factoring Fast Check: Before factoring ax² + bx + c, check if b² − 4ac is a perfect square. If yes → factors cleanly. If not → use the quadratic formula and save time not trying to force a factoring that won't work.

✏️ Quadratic Deep Practice — 6 SAT-Style Questions

Quadratic Question 1 of 6 — Factoring
What are the solutions to 2x² − 7x − 15 = 0?
✅ Answer: B — x = 5 and x = −3/2
Factor by grouping: 2x² − 7x − 15 = 0
• Find numbers that multiply to (2)(−15) = −30 AND add to −7 → 3 and −10
• Rewrite: 2x² + 3x − 10x − 15 = 0
• Group: x(2x + 3) − 5(2x + 3) = 0
• Factor: (x − 5)(2x + 3) = 0
• Solutions: x = 5 or x = −3/2 ✓
Quadratic Formula check: x = [7 ± √(49+120)] / 4 = [7 ± 13] / 4 → x = 5 or x = −3/2 ✓
❌ Why not A (x=3 and x=−5/2)? Test x=3: 2(9) − 21 − 15 = 18 − 36 = −18 ≠ 0. That's NOT a solution. Always verify by substituting.
Quadratic Question 2 of 6 — Vertex Form Minimum
The function f(x) = 2(x − 3)² + 5 has a minimum value. What is the minimum value and at what x does it occur?
✅ Answer: B — Minimum of 5, at x = 3
Vertex form: f(x) = a(x − h)² + k → Vertex = (h, k)
Here: f(x) = 2(x − 3)² + 5
• a = 2 (positive → opens UP → vertex is MINIMUM)
• h = 3 (the x in parentheses is x − 3, so h = +3)
• k = 5 (the constant added outside)
Minimum value = 5, occurs at x = 3 ✓
The minimum value is the OUTPUT (y-value) at the vertex, which is k = 5.
❌ Why not D (x = −3)? The (x − 3)² term means h = +3, NOT −3. If it were (x + 3)², then h = −3. The sign inside the parentheses is OPPOSITE to h.
❌ Why not minimum of 2? a = 2 is the coefficient that controls width of the parabola, not the minimum value. The minimum value is always k, the constant added OUTSIDE the parentheses.
Quadratic Question 3 of 6 — Discriminant
For what value of k does x² + kx + 16 = 0 have exactly one real solution?
✅ Answer: B — k = 8 or k = −8
Exactly one real solution → discriminant = 0
b² − 4ac = 0
Here: a = 1, b = k, c = 16
k² − 4(1)(16) = 0
k² − 64 = 0
k² = 64
k = 8 or k = −8 ✓
Check k = 8: x² + 8x + 16 = (x+4)² → one solution x = −4 ✓
Check k = −8: x² − 8x + 16 = (x−4)² → one solution x = 4 ✓
❌ Why not k = 4? k = 4: discriminant = 16 − 64 = −48 < 0 → NO real solutions. k = 4 gives zero solutions, not one.
Quadratic Question 4 of 6 — Projectile Maximum
A ball is thrown upward. Its height h(t) = −16t² + 64t + 5 feet, where t is in seconds. What is the maximum height the ball reaches?
✅ Answer: B — 69 feet
a = −16, b = 64, c = 5. Since a < 0, parabola opens DOWN → vertex is MAXIMUM
Step 1 — Find t at maximum: t = −b/(2a) = −64/(2×(−16)) = −64/−32 = 2 seconds
Step 2 — Find the maximum height:
h(2) = −16(2²) + 64(2) + 5
h(2) = −16(4) + 128 + 5
h(2) = −64 + 128 + 5 = 69 feet ✓
❌ Why not 64? 64 is the initial velocity (coefficient of t), not the height. The maximum height requires substituting t = 2 into the full equation.
❌ Why not 5? 5 is the starting height (h₀ when t = 0). The ball goes UP from 5 feet, reaching 69 feet at t = 2.
Quadratic Question 5 of 6 — Completing the Square
Which of the following is equivalent to x² − 6x + 5 = 0 written in vertex form, and what are the solutions?
✅ Answer: B — (x − 3)² = 4; solutions x = 5 or x = 1
Completing the square on x² − 6x + 5 = 0:
x² − 6x = −5
Add (6/2)² = 9 to both sides:
x² − 6x + 9 = −5 + 9
(x − 3)² = 4
x − 3 = ±2
x = 3 + 2 = 5 or x = 3 − 2 = 1 ✓
Factoring check: (x−5)(x−1) = 0 → x = 5 or x = 1 ✓
❌ Why not D ((x−3)² = 9)? When moving c = 5 to the right, we get −5, not 0. Then adding 9: −5 + 9 = 4, not 9. The right side is 4, not 9.
Quadratic Question 6 of 6 — Advanced: Roots and Coefficients
A quadratic function f(x) = a(x − r₁)(x − r₂) has roots at x = −2 and x = 5, and passes through the point (0, −20). What is the value of a?
✅ Answer: B — a = 2
Step 1 — Write factored form: f(x) = a(x − (−2))(x − 5) = a(x + 2)(x − 5)
Step 2 — Use the point (0, −20):
f(0) = a(0 + 2)(0 − 5) = a(2)(−5) = −10a
Set equal to −20: −10a = −20 → a = 2 ✓
Full equation: f(x) = 2(x+2)(x−5) = 2x² − 6x − 20
Check: f(0) = −20 ✓, f(−2) = 0 ✓, f(5) = 0 ✓
❌ Why not a = −2? With a = −2: f(0) = −2(2)(−5) = +20, not −20. The sign matters — check by substituting the full point.

Graph Challenge 1 of 3
Graph y = x² − 4x − 12 in the Quadratic Explorer. What are the zeros (x-intercepts)?
✅ Answer: B — x = 6 and x = −2
Factor: x² − 4x − 12 = (x − 6)(x + 2) = 0 → x = 6 or x = −2 ✓
In the graph: the parabola crosses the x-axis at x = −2 and x = 6.
The vertex is at x = (6 + (−2))/2 = 2, and the min = (2)² − 4(2) − 12 = 4 − 8 − 12 = −16.
Graph Challenge 2 of 3
Graph y = 2(x − 3)² + 5 using the Vertex Form preset. What does the graph tell you WITHOUT doing any algebra?
✅ Answer: B
From vertex form y = 2(x−3)²+5: vertex = (3, 5), a = 2 > 0 (opens up)
Since the minimum value is 5 (> 0) and the parabola opens UP, it NEVER crosses the x-axis.
The discriminant confirms: b²−4ac. Expanded: 2x²−12x+23. Discriminant = 144 − 4(2)(23) = 144 − 184 = −40 < 0 → no real zeros ✓
Graph Challenge 3 of 3
Graph h(t) = −16t² + 64t + 5 using the Projectile preset. From the graph, what is the maximum height and approximately when does the ball land?
✅ Answer: B
Vertex at t = −64/(2×(−16)) = 2. h(2) = −64 + 128 + 5 = 69 feet ✓
Ball lands when h(t) = 0: Using quadratic formula: t = [−64 ± √(4096 + 320)] / (−32) ≈ 4.08 seconds
The graph shows the parabola's right zero between t = 4 and t = 5.

🔗 Part B — Analogy Questions (SAT-Style)

Analogies test your understanding of word relationships. The format is: Word A : Word B :: Word C : ? — find what ? is to Word C as Word B is to Word A.

💡 ANALOGY STRATEGY: First, write a precise sentence connecting A and B. Then apply that EXACT same relationship to C : ?
Example: "CONCEDE is to admit what you LOST as _____ is to prove you WON" → REFUTE
Analogy 1 of 4
REFUTE : DISPROVE :: CORROBORATE : ___
Refute means to disprove something. Corroborate means to…
✅ Answer: B — Confirm
Relationship: REFUTE means to actively DISPROVE something (negative direction).
CORROBORATE is the OPPOSITE direction — it means to actively CONFIRM or support with evidence.
So: REFUTE:DISPROVE :: CORROBORATE:CONFIRM ✓
They are antonyms in the "direction" of support: refute goes against; corroborate goes with.
Analogy 2 of 4
SARDONIC : HUMOR :: DIDACTIC : ___
Sardonic describes a type of humor (dark/mocking). Didactic describes a type of…
✅ Answer: B — Teaching or moralizing
Sardonic is an adjective describing a specific TYPE of humor (dark, mocking).
Didactic is an adjective describing a specific TYPE of instructional intent — the intent to TEACH or moralize, often excessively.
The relationship: SARDONIC:HUMOR = a modifier describing a type of that thing. DIDACTIC:TEACHING = same structure.
Analogy 3 of 4
ANOMALY : DEVIATION :: PARADIGM : ___
An anomaly IS a deviation (from the expected). A paradigm IS a…
✅ Answer: C — Framework or model
ANOMALY is a synonym for DEVIATION (the two words mean essentially the same thing).
PARADIGM is a synonym for FRAMEWORK or MODEL — a standard pattern or structure that organizes thinking in a field.
The analogy type: word X = synonym for Y. ANOMALY = DEVIATION :: PARADIGM = FRAMEWORK ✓
Analogy 4 of 4
PRAGMATIC : REALISTIC :: AMBIVALENT : ___
Pragmatic means realistic/practical in approach. Ambivalent means…
✅ Answer: B — Having mixed feelings
PRAGMATIC: REALISTIC — pragmatic essentially means realistic (dealing with things as they are).
AMBIVALENT: HAVING MIXED FEELINGS — ambivalent essentially means having contradictory or mixed feelings.
The structure: adjective → its core definition. PRAGMATIC = REALISTIC :: AMBIVALENT = MIXED FEELINGS ✓
